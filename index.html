Parking

We have street parking, but we live on a small narrow street and parking can get crowded so we recommend taking public transportation (see below) or a taxi/Uber/Lyft/etc.

Public Transportation

We live right along the #2 bus line, just a couple blocks from the 3, 4, and 48 bus lines, and a slightly longer but pleasant walk from the 10, 11, and 12 bus lines.

Hotels

These hotels are close to us. If you want to explore more of Seattle please research hotels in other neighborhoods, and feel free to ask us for any advice on transportation/activities, etc.

  • First Hill: The Sorrento Hotel is a beautiful historic hotel with ornate architecture and a lovely cocktail service in the lobby area. Walking distance to downtown.
  • Capitol Hill: The Silver Cloud Hotel on Broadway is right along Broadway at the south end of Seattle’s lively gay neighborhood with plentiful restaurants and nightlife.
  • Downtown: Hotel Max is steps from Pike Place Market and Capitol Hill, and in the middle of all the music action. Additionally, any of the major national hotel chains are available downtown, and are a short bus or taxi ride from our home.

Registry

We aren’t registered anywhere, but we would love it if you donated to any of our favorite non-profits:

For the environment

Nature Consortium (Seattle, WA) is a grassroots, community-based organization whose mission is to connect people, arts and nature. They produce a Youth Art Program, an Urban Forest Restoration Program and the Arts in Nature Festival. Donate Here

For animals

Pigs Peace Sanctuary (Stanwood, WA) is a facility helping animals and educating people since 1994. They are specialized in pigs but also have welcomed other animals in need and are currently home to ponies, horses, llamas, dogs, cats, chickens, sheep and turkeys. 100% of donations go for the care of the animals who call the sanctuary home. Donation Form Here

VegWorcester (Worcester, MA) is a not-for-profit, community organization that advocates vegetarian living to the population of Central Massachusetts. Veg Worcester works to create a more peaceful world by encouraging people to think twice about the suffering inherent in eating animals. They focus on building a culture of support for vegetarianism.Donate Here

For community

ANEW (Apprenticeship & Nontraditional Employment for Women) (Renton, WA) links women to apprenticeships and liveable wage jobs in construction trades, manufacturing, aerospace and utilities. ANEW supports women and men of all ages and backgrounds, and the apprenticeships and employers who advocate for their success.Donate Here

Casa Latina's (Seattle, WA) mission is to empower Latino immigrants through educational and economic opportunities. Their vision is that the Latino community participate fully in the economy and democracy of this country. Their values are social justice, community, respect, democracy, integrity, and learning.Donate Here

AtWork! (Bellevue, WA) is a highly supportive and innovative conduit between people with disabilities and mainstream employers in the community. Donate Here

Allyship (Seattle, WA) believes that all oppressions are interconnected and in the possibility of a world free of oppression. Allyship seeks to work in solidarity with marginalized communities to understand the interconnectedness of oppression and to support the systematic liberation of all people.Donate Here
